Work & Education

Current Role

At present, I'm a Monetisation and LiveOps Content Developer at Jagex - which is a lofty title for a cross between a designer and developer.

I work as part of a cross-functional team to create products which engage and retain consumers. I'm passionate about data-driven and consumer-centred design, and work closely with both our users and our in-house data science team to design products and promotions that meet our players' needs.

Diversity and inclusion are important to me - since joining Jagex I've been an active member of the company's Diversity and LGBT+ groups, pushing for cultural change within both the workplace and our products.

Previous Work

In the past, I've worked in both software-related and non-software related roles. I've worked in Science Gallery Dublin as a mediator, where I spent my days explaining scient and art to people of all ages and backgrounds. I've tutored kids in the fundamentals of programming and game design.

I spent a year working as a mobile software developer in a very early-stage startup with only a handful of people, where I got to see first-hand the struggles of trying to find funding and garner interest in a product. My core role was developing a React Native based application for Android and iOS. It was as part of this role that I first developed a passion for UI/UX design, attending several conferences and visiting universities to contribute to their UX courses.

Education

I have a masters in Computer Engineering from Trinity College Dublin.

Throughout my degree, I gained academic experience developing programs for various platforms, using languages such as C++, C#, Java, Haskell and Python, and a range of applications including Unreal Engine 4, Unity, MATLAB and Maya.

I have written two dissertations; the first explored methods of rendering realistic digital humans in virtual reality environments, the second - which you can find here - investigated users' agency over search engine learning through the development of a browser plug-in which detected, assessed and disrupted that learning.
